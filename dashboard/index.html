Spatial Mismatch Hypothesis

The Spatial Mismatch Hypothesis argues that unemployment and lower wages can result when workers, especially low-income and historically marginalized residents, live far from growing job centers and face housing or transportation barriers that limit access to those opportunities.

This atlas operationalizes the concept with tract-level indicators for jobs, wage tiers, resident workers, affordability, transit proximity, vehicle access, and neighborhood socioeconomic context.

Sources: Census TIGERweb, LEHD/LODES, ACS 5-year where available, and GTFS when supplied.
